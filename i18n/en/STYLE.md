# English Translation Style Guide — HongKong.md

## Core Principle

> Rewrite, don't translate. Every English article should read as if it were originally written in English.

## Terminology Table

| Chinese | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| 香港 | Hong Kong | Never "Hongkong" (colonial-era spelling) |
| 廣東話 / 粵語 | Cantonese | Acceptable for both spoken and written; specify "written Cantonese" when needed |
| 飲茶 | yum cha / dim sum | "Yum cha" = the act of going for tea; "dim sum" = the dishes |
| 茶餐廳 | cha chaan teng | Italicise on first use; gloss as "Hong Kong–style café" |
| 回歸 | Handover | Capitalise when referring to the 1997 event |
| 基本法 | Basic Law | Always capitalised |
| 立法會 | Legislative Council (LegCo) | Abbreviation "LegCo" acceptable after first full mention |
| 行政長官 | Chief Executive | Never "governor" post-1997 |
| 港鐵 | MTR | No need to expand; universally understood |
| 新界 | New Territories | Always capitalised |
| 離島 | Outlying Islands | Capitalised as a district name |
| 大排檔 | dai pai dong | Italicise on first use; gloss as "open-air street-food stall" |
| 原住民 | Indigenous peoples | Capitalise "Indigenous"; prefer "Indigenous peoples" over "aborigines" |

## Cultural-Context Supplements

When translating culturally specific concepts, add brief context:

- **一國兩制** → "One Country, Two Systems" (the constitutional framework governing Hong Kong's relationship with mainland China)
- **功夫** → Keep as "kung fu" — internationally recognised
- **粵劇** → "Cantonese opera" — briefly describe as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage
- **紅白二事** → Explain separately: "wedding banquets (紅事)" and "funeral customs (白事)"
- **HKD / 港幣** → Convert to USD equivalent when helpful: "HK$100 (≈ US$13)"

## Tone & Register

- Follow AP Style for general guidance.
- Conversational but authoritative — aim for *The Economist* or *South China Morning Post* feature writing.
- Avoid academic jargon; explain specialist terms on first use.
- Use British English spelling conventions (colour, centre, organisation) — consistent with Hong Kong usage.

## Common Errors

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| "a Chinese city" | "a city in China's Hong Kong SAR" or simply "Hong Kong" | Hong Kong's status is unique under "One Country, Two Systems" |
| "Mainland" alone | "mainland China" or "the mainland" (lowercase) | Needs the noun for clarity in English |
| "aboriginal" | "Indigenous" | Respectful, modern usage |
| "Fragrant Harbour" | "Hong Kong" | Avoid clichéd literal translations |
| "Red China" | "the People's Republic of China" or "mainland China" | Neutral, factual language |
| Colonial nostalgia framing | Balanced historical narrative | Present history factually without romanticising any era |
